Seasonal care at a glance

How your plants behave through Indian summer, monsoon, and winter.

Summer

Heat & strong light

  • Shift delicate plants away from harsh afternoon sun.
  • Water in the early morning or evening, not mid-day.
  • Check balcony plants like Tulsi and Adenium more often.
Monsoon

Moist air & wet soil

  • Cut back watering‚Äîsoil stays wet longer.
  • Watch for fungus in pots with poor drainage.
  • Succulents like Aloe and Adenium hate soggy roots.
Winter

Slow growth & rest

  • Most plants pause growth; don‚Äôt over-fertilize.
  • Let soil dry more between waterings.
  • Move tropical plants away from cold windows at night.

Care basics for almost every plant

Four pillars that keep 90% of common indoor and balcony plants happy.

Light

Most plants prefer bright, indirect light. Balcony herbs and Adenium enjoy more direct sun than indoor foliage.

Water

When in doubt, underwater a little instead of overwatering. Always check the top soil with your finger first.

Soil & pot

Use a well-draining mix and a pot with a drainage hole. Standing water at the bottom is the fastest way to root rot.

Air & humidity

Good airflow prevents fungus and pests. Keep balcony plants slightly spaced out so leaves can fully dry.
