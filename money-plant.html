Money Plant (Pothos)

A classic trailing vine that survives low light, missed waterings and busy student life ‚Äî perfect for shelves, windows and hanging pots.

About this plant

Money Plant (often called Pothos) is one of the easiest indoor plants to grow. It has heart-shaped leaves that can be plain green, neon or variegated, and it happily trails from shelves, windows or hanging baskets.

It tolerates low light, dry air and occasional neglect, which makes it a favourite for hostels, study rooms and offices. With better light and regular care, it grows quickly and can be propagated easily in water or soil.

Care requirements

Light

Low to bright, indirect light. Grows faster and bushier in brighter spots, but can survive in low light.

Water

Water when the top 2‚Äì3 cm of soil feels dry. Slight underwatering is safer than overwatering.

Soil

Normal potting mix with good drainage works well. Add a bit of perlite or sand if soil holds too much water.

Propagation

Very easy to propagate from stem cuttings in water or directly in soil.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Active growth; may need more frequent watering in very hot rooms.
  • Monsoon: Soil stays wet longer; check soil before watering to avoid sogginess.
  • Winter: Growth slows; water less often and keep away from very cold windows.

Quick info

  • Scientific name: Epipremnum aureum
  • Type: Trailing indoor vine
  • Care level: Very easy
  • Ideal spot: Shelf, window, hanging pot
  • Extras: Easy propagation, great for beginners
