Plant Profile

Snake Plant

One of the toughest indoor plants—loves low light, survives neglect, and purifies the air.

About the Snake Plant

The Snake Plant (also known as Sansevieria or Mother-in-law’s Tongue) is famously one of the easiest houseplants to care for. Its upright, sword-like leaves make it perfect for bedrooms, offices, and corners with minimal sunlight.

Known for its air-purifying properties, the Snake Plant naturally filters toxins and improves indoor air quality. It thrives even with occasional watering and can tolerate both bright and low light conditions.

Care Requirements

Light

Tolerates low light but grows best in bright, indirect light.

Water

Water every 2–3 weeks. Let soil dry completely between waterings.

Soil & Pot

Dry, sandy, well-draining soil. Avoid letting roots stay wet.

Pet Safety

Not pet-safe. Can cause mild stomach upset if eaten.

Seasonal Behavior

  • Summer: Water weekly if very hot.
  • Monsoon: Reduce watering → soil stays damp longer.
  • Winter: Water only once every 4 weeks.

Quick Info

  • Scientific Name: Sansevieria trifasciata
  • Type: Indoor foliage
  • Care Level: Very easy
  • Ideal Room: Bedroom, office, living room
  • Growth Speed: Slow to moderate
  • Mature Height: 1–4 feet indoors
