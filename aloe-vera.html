Plant Profile

Aloe Vera

A healing succulent loved for its medicinal gel and low-maintenance care.

About Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera is a classic succulent known for its thick, fleshy leaves that store water and contain soothing gel. This gel is widely used in skincare, burns, and healing products. Aloe is extremely forgiving and thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Originating from desert-like regions, Aloe Vera prefers warmth and lots of sunlight. It is one of the most drought-tolerant houseplants, making it ideal for busy students, beginners, and minimal-care homes.

Care Requirements

Light

Needs bright sunlight. Best on a sunny windowsill.

Water

Water once every 2–3 weeks. Let soil dry completely.

Soil & Pot

Cactus/succulent soil. Use pots with drainage holes.

Pet Safety

Not safe for pets if eaten.

Seasonal Behavior

  • Summer: Fast growth; water more often.
  • Monsoon: Avoid overwatering; soil gets damp longer.
  • Winter: Growth slows; water only once every 4–5 weeks.

Quick Info

  • Scientific Name: Aloe barbadensis miller
  • Type: Succulent
  • Care Level: Easy
  • Ideal Room: Balcony, windowsill
  • Growth Speed: Slow to moderate
  • Mature Height: 1–2 feet indoors
