Plant Profile

Bougainvillea

A bold, vibrant climber known for its papery, colourful bracts that bloom almost all year long.

About this plant

Bougainvillea is a hardy, sun-loving flowering plant famous for its bright magenta, pink, orange, white, and red blooms. What we call ‚Äòflowers‚Äô are actually colourful bracts that surround tiny white flowers in the centre.

It grows vigorously as a climber, arching shrub, or even potted plant if pruned regularly. Bougainvillea thrives in dry conditions and rewards you with continuous, dazzling blooms.

Care requirements

Light

Needs full sunlight (6‚Äì8 hours daily) for heavy blooming.

Water

Water sparingly; drought-tolerant plant. Overwatering reduces flowering.

Soil

Well-draining sandy or loamy soil; avoid clay-heavy soil.

Pruning

Prune after flowering to shape and encourage new growth.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Blooms heavily; water moderately.
  • Monsoon: Reduce watering; avoid soggy soil.
  • Winter: Flowering slows; protect from frost and cold winds.

Quick info

  • Scientific name: Bougainvillea glabra
  • Type: Flowering climber
  • Care level: Easy
  • Ideal spot: Terrace, boundary wall, or sunny balcony
  • Uses: Decoration, landscaping, hedges
