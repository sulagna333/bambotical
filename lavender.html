About this plant

Lavender is a classic herb grown for its fragrance and soft purple blooms. It prefers cool roots, lots of light, and soil that never stays soggy.

It can be a little fussy in very humid climates, but with the right potting mix and enough sun, it becomes a beautiful feature plant on balconies and terraces.

Care requirements

Light

Needs strong light; aim for 5‚Äì6 hours of sun each day.

Water

Water deeply but rarely; let soil dry well between watering.

Soil

Use a very well-draining mix with sand or gravel.

Airflow

Good airflow helps prevent fungus in humid climates.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Peak blooming season; keep watered but never soggy.
  • Monsoon: Protect from constant rain; move under a roof if needed.
  • Winter: Growth slows; give as much sunlight as possible.

Quick info

  • Scientific name: Lavandula (various species)
  • Type: Flowering herb
  • Care level: Medium
  • Ideal spot: Sunny balcony or terrace edge
  • Uses: Fragrance, sachets, simple decor
