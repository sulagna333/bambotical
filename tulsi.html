About this plant

Tulsi, or Holy Basil, is a highly valued herb in Indian culture. It is used in herbal teas, home remedies, and daily rituals. It grows best in a warm balcony or sunny window with regular care.

The plant prefers consistent moisture but does not like to stay in soggy soil. With enough sun and pruning, Tulsi bushes out nicely and stays productive for a long time.

Light

Water

Keep soil lightly moist; water when the top layer feels dry.

Soil

Use a loose, well-draining potting mix with compost.

Harvest

Pinch leaves regularly from the top to encourage branching.

Seasonal behavior

  • Summer: Thrives with sun and regular watering; may need more frequent watering in heat.
  • Monsoon: Reduce watering slightly; watch for fungus and pests on wet leaves.
  • Winter: Growth slows; protect from cold winds and water less often.
